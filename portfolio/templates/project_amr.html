My final year project was the design and development of an Autonomous Mobile Robot with an automated devlivery mechanism for use in warehouses. For this project, I worked in the Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineering (R.I.S.E) Lab under the supervision of Dr. Khawaja Fahad Iqbal.

The robot made use of a differential wheel drive for locomotion. The automated delivery mechanism used automated rollers which detected the presence of the object at pickup, loaded them to the center of the platform, and dropped them off at the designated endpoint.

My part in the project was to focus on the concept development, and programming of the mobile robot. For obstacle detection, the robot used ultrasonic sensors. For distance tracking, wheel encoders were used. IR sensors were used for delivery object tracking. Arduino was used as the processing unit for this project.

The results of the project were presented and published at the Applied Human Factors and Ergonomics Conference (2022) titled A Chain-Driven Live Roller Mechanism for Loading and Unloading Packages on Autonomous Mobile Robots in Warehouses. I was responsible for the technical writing of the paper, and was designated as the primary author of the paper.
